How a universal system of assessment can change the world

When people think of education, they do not first think of the humble test. Instead their thoughts will turn to the classroom, or perhaps to teachers, textbooks, or curricula. But while an assessment may be distinct from learning, it is fundamental to the process of education as a whole. 

So when we tell people that we’re building a radically new system of assessment, it takes a while for them to connect it to our master plan for Universal Education, but it becomes clear once you map it out.

Education is the recursive system of learning by operating on learning. You can only operate on that which can be measured. So, to create a universal education system, you need to have a universal assessment system that can measure anything, for anyone. 

That’s pretty damn hard to do.
